# Design context for spots

## Design Context

### Users
People in Addis Ababa (often couples) deciding where to go on a date, browsing
spots sourced from TikTok review channels. Usage is casual and discovery-driven,
frequently on a phone during downtime — they want to skim a spot, see where it
is and what it costs, mark places they've been, rate them, and save ones they
want to go to. Identity is via Google sign-in for the personal log.

### Brand Personality
Warm & editorial — a curated city guide a friend made for you. Personal,
inviting, magazine-like; not corporate, not techy. Voice uses "we/our"
("Places we've been", "our notes", "our rating").

### Aesthetic Direction
Light theme (warm cream surfaces, deep green + terracotta/amber accents already
in the palette). Editorial warmth over slickness. Quicksand as the UI typeface
(rounded, soft). Playful touches exist and are welcome (dice "Surprise me",
Tinder-style swipe, confetti/implosion on rating extremes) but should stay
tasteful and not overwhelm the editorial calm. Anti-reference: generic SaaS
dashboard, AI-slop (cyan-on-dark, glassmorphism-everywhere, purple gradients).

### Design Principles
1. **Warm, not clinical** — tinted neutrals, no pure black/white, color used sparingly for accent.
2. **Mobile-first reality** — most use is on a phone; touch targets and one-handed flows matter.
3. **Editorial hierarchy** — clear type hierarchy; the spot is the hero, chrome recedes.
4. **Delight in moments** — a few well-placed flourishes (dice, swipe, confetti), not scattered everywhere.
5. **Accessibility: WCAG AA** — AA contrast, keyboard operability, labelled controls.
